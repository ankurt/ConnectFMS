About ConnectFMS

ConnectFMS is designed to break the communication barrier between Carnegie Mellon students & faculty, and the staff at Facilities Management Services with regards to campus energy usage.

The Center for Building Performance and Diagnostics (CBPD) at the School of Architecture served as a client for the Software Development Project course (67-373) offered by the Information Systems department. Our team was assigned to develop a technological solution for our client's need to engage the campus community to conserve energy.

We surveyed over 240+ students and 50+ staff/faculty to gauge the interest of the CMU body on issues related to campus energy consumption and future efforts to conserve energy on-campus. After presenting our results to CBPD and two FMS representatives, Marty and Steve, we concluded that there is a substantial disconnect between the student/faculty body and FMS, where information regarding campus energy usage or efforts to make the campus 'more green' are not made aware to the general CMU community.

This is where ConnectFMS comes into play. Through this applications, students, faculty and staff can make posts on energy conservation issues they see around campus by taking a photo and writing a short description. Students, faculty and staff can consequently upvote/downvote on others' posts. Registered FMS staff will be able to view and address issues made through these posts and make statuses on campus initiatives to go green.
